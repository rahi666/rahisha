LIFE & CAREER

Background: Born on January 16, 1996, in Bundang, South Korea. She moved to New Zealand at age nine to study, returning in 2010 to pursue music instead of a planned move to the US to study law.

YG Trainee: Auditioned for YG Entertainment in 2010. Due to her English fluency and versatility, she was trained as a rapper. Before debuting, she gained major exposure starring in G-Dragon's "That XX" music video (2012) and featuring on tracks by Lee Hi, Seungri, and G-Dragon.

Blackpink Debut: Revealed as the first member of Blackpink, debuting on August 8, 2016, with Square One ("Boombayah" / "Whistle"). She took on a core decision-making role within the group.

Variety & "Solo": Became a major variety TV star in 2018. In November 2018, she released her record-breaking single "Solo", achieving a Gaon triple crown and topping the Billboard World Digital Songs chart.

Coachella (2019): Became the first Korean solo artist to perform at Coachella during Blackpink's historic set.

Acting Debut: Starred under the stage name Jennie Ruby Jane in HBO's The Idol (2023). Her soundtrack collaboration, "One of the Girls" with The Weeknd, crossed 1 billion Spotify streams and went RIAA Platinum.

Odd Atelier & MBE: Invested as an Honorary Member of the Order of the British Empire (MBE) by King Charles III in late 2023. She renewed her group contract with YG but founded her own solo label, Odd Atelier, for individual projects.

Chart Success: Released the hit special single "You & Me" (2023) and scored a domestic number-one hit with Zico on the single "Spot!" (2024), winning four awards at the 2024 MAMA Awards.

Debut Studio Album: Signed globally with Columbia Records and released her highly successful debut album, Ruby, on March 7, 2025. Backed by hit singles like "Mantra" and "Like Jennie," it sold one million copies in its first week and reached number three on the UK Albums Chart.

Record Breaker: Became the first female K-pop soloist to chart three tracks simultaneously on the Billboard Hot 100.

Awards & Touring: Launched The Ruby Experience concert tour, historically became the first soloist to win Record of the Year at the Melon Music Awards, and clinched Artist of the Year at the 2026 Golden Disc Awards.

Recent Projects: Scored her first Billboard Hot 100 top-ten hit (peaking at number 8) in 2026 via a massive collaborative remix of Tame Impala's "Dracula." She capped off the summer by headlining major Western festivals, including the Governors Ball, Roskilde, and Open'er back-to-back.
