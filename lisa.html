Career

Background: Born Pranpriya Manobal on March 27, 1997, in Buriram, Thailand, she later legally changed her name to Lalisa. She began dancing at age four and competed in elite dance crews like We Zaa Cool.

YG Trainee: In 2010, she was the sole qualifier out of 4,000 applicants at YG Entertainment's Thailand auditions, moving to South Korea in 2011 as the label's first non-ethnic Korean trainee.

Group Debut: Debuted as Blackpink's main dancer and rapper on August 8, 2016, immediately reaching historic heights.

Solo Records (Lalisa & "Money"): Released her debut solo single album Lalisa in 2021. The title track broke the global 24-hour YouTube view record for a soloist (73.6 million views), while the B-side "Money" became a massive viral phenomenon, becoming the first solo female K-pop song to cross 1 billion Spotify streams.

Award-Winning Trailblazer: Made history as the first K-pop solo artist to win Best K-Pop at both the MTV VMAs and EMAs. She ultimately amassed eight Guinness World Records, making her the most-decorated K-pop soloist in history.

Label Management: Invested as an MBE by King Charles III in late 2023. In 2024, she left YG for solo work, launched her own management company, Lloud, and partnered with RCA Records.

Debut Studio Album Alter Ego (2025): Released her debut studio album Alter Ego on February 28, 2025, yielding massive global hits like "Rockstar" (which topped the Billboard Global Excl. US chart), "New Woman" featuring Rosalía, and "Burn Again" featuring Doja Cat and Raye.

Acting & Hollywood Push: Made her acting debut under her given name Lalisa Manobal as Mook in Season 3 of HBO's The White Lotus (2025). She expanded her Hollywood presence by securing roles in Netflix's action spin-off Tycho and an upcoming romantic comedy film.

Historic Milestones: In 2026, she became the first K-pop artist to secure a Las Vegas concert residency (Viva La Liza at Caesars Palace) and made history as the first female K-pop act to headline a FIFA World Cup opening ceremony, performing the official soundtrack single "Goals" in Los Angeles.
