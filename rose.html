CAREER

Background: Born Rosanne Park (Park Chae-young) on February 11, 1997, in New Zealand to South Korean parents, she raised and spent her childhood in Melbourne, Australia.

YG Trainee: At her father's suggestion, she auditioned for YG Entertainment in Sydney in 2012, beating out 700 applicants. She dropped out of high school to move to Seoul, featuring uncredited on G-Dragon's track "Without You" that same year.

Group Debut: Trained for four years before debuting as the final member of Blackpink on August 8, 2016, rapidly achieving massive group fame.

Solo Single Album R (2021): Her debut solo project R featured the smash hits "On the Ground" and "Gone." She broke the record for the highest first-week sales by a Korean female soloist and earned two Guinness World Records after topping both Billboard Global charts as a soloist and group member.

Label Independence: Signed with The Black Label for management and Atlantic Records for her solo music after electing not to renew her individual contract with YG.

The "APT." Phenomenon: In late 2024, she dropped the monumental collaboration "APT." with Bruno Mars. The song shattered records globally, peaking at number three on the US Billboard Hot 100, surpassing 1 billion Spotify streams in just 100 days, and becoming the IFPI Best-Selling Global Single of 2025.

Debut Album Rosie: Released on December 6, 2024, her debut studio album peaked at number three on the Billboard 200 and number four on the UK Albums Chart, setting new heights for female K-pop solo artists.

Historic Award Sweep:

  • 2025: Won Song of the Year at the MTV VMAs for "APT." and scored three historic Grammy nominations—becoming the first K-pop lead artist nominated in a "Big Four" category (Record and Song of the Year).
  • 2026: Won International Song of the Year at the Brit Awards, making her the first K-pop artist in history to win at the ceremony.
